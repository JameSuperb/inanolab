Kirchhoff's Laws

Current Law & Voltage Law — Interactive Physics Lesson

Why Do We Need Kirchhoff's Laws?

Ohm's Law (V = IR) works great for simple circuits with one resistor and one battery. But real circuits have junctions where wires split, and multiple loops with different paths for current. Ohm's Law alone can't tell us how current divides at a junction or how voltage distributes across complex loops.

Gustav Kirchhoff gave us two powerful rules that work for any circuit, no matter how complex.

Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)

At any junction (node) in a circuit, the total current flowing in equals the total current flowing out.

ΣI_in = ΣI_out

Based on conservation of charge — charge cannot appear or disappear at a point.

Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)

Around any closed loop in a circuit, the sum of all voltage gains (from batteries) minus all voltage drops (across resistors) equals zero.

ΣV_rises - ΣV_drops = 0

Based on conservation of energy — energy gained equals energy lost in a complete loop.

Practice Problem 1 — Two-Loop Circuit

Given: V₁ = 12 V  |  V₂ = 6 V  |  R₁ = 4 Ω  |  R₂ = 6 Ω  |  R₃ = 2 Ω (shared)
Assume clockwise mesh currents: I₁ (left loop), I₂ (right loop)

Q1. Solve for I₁ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Q2. Solve for I₂ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Q3. Find I₃ = I₁ − I₂, the current through shared R₃ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Step 1 — KVL Equations

Left loop: 12 − 4I₁ − 2(I₁−I₂) = 0 → 12 − 6I₁ + 2I₂ = 0
→ 6I₁ − 2I₂ = 12  …(1)
Right loop: 6 − 6I₂ − 2(I₂−I₁) = 0 → 6 + 2I₁ − 8I₂ = 0
→ 2I₁ − 8I₂ = −6  …(2)

Step 2 — Solve (Cramer's Rule)

Matrix: [6, −2 ; −2, 8] · [I₁ ; I₂] = [12 ; 6]
det = (6)(8) − (−2)(−2) = 48 − 4 = 44
I₁ = (12×8 − 6×(−2)) / 44 = 108 / 44 ≈ 2.45 A
I₂ = (6×6 − 12×(−2)) / 44 = 60 / 44 ≈ 1.36 A

Step 3 — Current through R₃

I₃ = I₁ − I₂ = 2.45 − 1.36 ≈ 1.09 A ↓ (downward)

Verification (KVL check)

Left: 12 − 2.45×4 − 1.09×2 = 0.000 ≈ 0 ✓
Right: 6 − 1.36×6 + 1.09×2 = 0.000 ≈ 0 ✓

Practice Problem 2 — Two-Loop Circuit

Given: V₁ = 18 V  |  V₂ = 9 V  |  R₁ = 3 Ω  |  R₂ = 5 Ω  |  R₃ = 4 Ω (shared)
Assume clockwise mesh currents: I₁ (left loop), I₂ (right loop)

Q1. Solve for I₁ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Q2. Solve for I₂ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Q3. Find I₃ = I₁ − I₂, the current through shared R₃ (Amperes, round to 2 decimal places).

Step 1 — KVL Equations

Left loop: 18 − 3I₁ − 4(I₁−I₂) = 0 → 18 − 7I₁ + 4I₂ = 0
→ 7I₁ − 4I₂ = 18  …(1)
Right loop: 9 − 5I₂ − 4(I₂−I₁) = 0 → 9 + 4I₁ − 9I₂ = 0
→ 4I₁ − 9I₂ = −9  …(2)

Step 2 — Solve (Cramer's Rule)

Matrix: [7, −4 ; −4, 9] · [I₁ ; I₂] = [18 ; 9]
det = (7)(9) − (−4)(−4) = 63 − 16 = 47
I₁ = (18×9 − 9×(−4)) / 47 = 198 / 47 ≈ 4.21 A
I₂ = (9×7 − 18×(−4)) / 47 = 135 / 47 ≈ 2.87 A

Step 3 — Current through R₃

I₃ = I₁ − I₂ = 4.21 − 2.87 ≈ 1.34 A ↓ (downward)

Verification (KVL check)

Left: 18 − 4.21×3 − 1.34×4 = 0.000 ≈ 0 ✓
Right: 9 − 2.87×5 + 1.34×4 = 0.000 ≈ 0 ✓
