⚡ Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law describes the relationship between Voltage (V), Current (I), and Resistance (R) in an electrical circuit. It was discovered by Georg Simon Ohm in 1827.

V = I × R   →   I = V / R   →   R = V / I

🔗 Series Connection

In a series circuit, resistors are connected one after another in a single path. The same current flows through each resistor, but the voltage is divided among them proportionally to their resistance.

Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃

🔑 Key Point: In series, current is the same everywhere, but voltage divides proportionally. The total of all voltage drops equals the source voltage (Kirchhoff's Voltage Law).

🔀 Parallel Connection

In a parallel circuit, resistors are connected in separate branches. Each branch has the same voltage across it, but the current divides among the branches — more current flows through lower-resistance paths.

1/Rtotal = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃

🔑 Key Point: In parallel, voltage is the same across all branches, but current divides. The total equivalent resistance is always less than the smallest individual resistor. (Kirchhoff's Current Law: total current = sum of branch currents.)

Ohm's Law

Problem 1: A light bulb has a resistance of 240Ω and is connected to a 120V supply. What current flows through it?

Given: V = 120 V, R = 240 Ω
Find: I = ?

Using Ohm's Law: I = V / R
I = 120 / 240
I = 0.5 A (500 mA)
Ohm's Law

Problem 2: A 2A current flows through a 15Ω resistor. What voltage is across the resistor?

Given: I = 2 A, R = 15 Ω
Find: V = ?

V = I × R = 2 × 15
V = 30 V
Series

Problem 3: Three resistors (47Ω, 100Ω, 220Ω) are in series with a 24V battery. Find the total resistance.

Given: R₁ = 47Ω, R₂ = 100Ω, R₃ = 220Ω, V = 24V

R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
R_total = 47 + 100 + 220
R_total = 367 Ω
Series

Problem 4: Two resistors in series have a total resistance of 500Ω. If R₁ = 180Ω, find R₂.

Given: R_total = 500Ω, R₁ = 180Ω

R₂ = R_total − R₁ = 500 − 180
R₂ = 320 Ω
Parallel

Problem 5: Two resistors (100Ω and 200Ω) are connected in parallel to a 10V supply. Find the equivalent resistance.

Given: R₁ = 100Ω, R₂ = 200Ω, V = 10V

1/R_eq = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
1/R_eq = 1/100 + 1/200 = 0.015
R_eq = 66.7 Ω
Parallel

Problem 6: Three identical 330Ω resistors are connected in parallel. Find the equivalent resistance.

Given: R₁ = R₂ = R₃ = 330Ω

R_eq = R / n = 330 / 3
R_eq = 110 Ω
