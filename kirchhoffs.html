Experimental errors are inevitable variations between a measurement and its true value. They are classified into two main categories: • Systematic Errors: These result in consistent, reproducible bias where measurements are always too high or too low. They generally stem from personal errors (e.g., observer bias, parallax) or external errors caused by environmental conditions like temperature changes or vibrations. • Random Errors: These are unpredictable variations caused by uncontrollable factors, resulting in data scattered above and below the true value. Their impact is reduced by taking repeated measurements. To analyze these errors, statistics are used to determine central tendency (finding the average value) and dispersion (using standard deviation to measure precision and reliability)
