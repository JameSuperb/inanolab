What's your subject?
Step 1: How many factors are you analyzing?
A factor is an independent variable you manipulate. For example: comparing different dosage of drug (one factor) or drug dosage AND temperature conditions (two factors).
Step 2: What best describes your study design?
Independent groups: Different subjects in each condition. e.g., 20 mice on Drug A, 20 different mice on Drug B
Paired/Matched: Same subjects twice, or matched pairs. e.g., plant height before and after treatment
Repeated: Same subjects multiple times. e.g., enzyme activity measured on Day 1, Day 7, Day 14
Step 2: What best describes the design?
Independent groups only: Both factors use completely different subjects.
Includes repeated measurements: At least one factor involves the same subjects measured multiple times.
Step 3: How many groups or conditions?
Count the distinct levels of your factor. (e.g., Control vs Treatment = 2; Low vs Medium vs High = 3)
Step 4: Is your data normally distributed?
Yes: Your measurements look roughly bell-shaped.

Statistical Test for Differences
Version: 1.1
Website: www.inanolab.com/statistics.html
Developer / Programmer: Dr. James Salveo Olarve
Affiliation: i-Nano Research Facility, De La Salle University Manila

This app supports common tests of difference, structured data entry, guided test selection, basic assumption checks, plots, and exportable result summaries. It is designed for fast laboratory or classroom analysis, not as a replacement for full statistical review.

Workflow

  • Use Get Started to follow the test recommendation guide.
  • Or select a test directly from the sidebar.
  • Load demo data, upload CSV, or enter values manually.
  • Click Run Test and inspect the Results tab.

Data Entry

  • Wide tables are used for Independent t-test, Mann-Whitney U, One-way ANOVA, and Kruskal-Wallis.
  • Matched or long layouts are used for paired, repeated, and two-factor designs.
  • Headers can be renamed to match your experiment labels.
  • CSV templates follow the active test format.

Outputs

  • Inferential test statistics and p-values
  • Effect sizes where implemented
  • Group summaries and plots
  • Assumptions, Q-Q plots, and post hoc sections when relevant

Supported Tests

Independent t-test: two independent groups under approximate normality.
Paired t-test: two related measurements from the same or matched units.
One-way ANOVA: three or more independent groups in a parametric design.
Two-way ANOVA: two independent factors in a parametric design.
Repeated Measures ANOVA: the same subjects measured across three or more conditions.
Mann-Whitney U: non-parametric alternative to the independent t-test.
Wilcoxon Signed-Rank: non-parametric alternative to the paired t-test.
Kruskal-Wallis: non-parametric alternative to one-way ANOVA.
Friedman Test: non-parametric alternative to repeated-measures ANOVA.

Results Sections

Test Statistics: the main inferential result, including statistic, degrees of freedom when applicable, p-value, and effect size when available.
Data Summary: descriptive statistics for each group.
Data Visualization: descriptive and box-and-whisker plots.
Assumptions: normality and homogeneity checks where appropriate.
Q-Q Plot: a visual normality check for the same scope used in the assumptions section.
Post Hoc Analysis: pairwise follow-up comparisons after significant omnibus tests where implemented.

Post Hoc Methods

One-way ANOVA: Tukey HSD or Tukey-Kramer for unequal group sizes.
Repeated Measures ANOVA: paired t-tests with Bonferroni adjustment.
Kruskal-Wallis: Dunn's test with Holm correction.
Two-way ANOVA: automated simple-effects post hoc testing is not implemented.

Important Notes

Normality: the app reports an approximate Shapiro-Wilk procedure based on simulation rather than a dedicated exact implementation.
Homogeneity: Levene's test is used for group variance homogeneity when relevant.
Interpretation: a small p-value suggests evidence against the null hypothesis, but scientific importance still depends on effect size, design quality, and context.
Exports: tables can be copied as TSV and plots or sections can be exported as PNG.
